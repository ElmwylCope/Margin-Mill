The problem

Reports exist, but the owner still lacks a clear operating read.

Most distributors already have accounting reports, ERP exports, sales data, and bank activity. The issue is that those numbers often explain what happened without showing what deserves attention first.

01

Cash looks fine until timing gets tight.

Cash pressure often shows up through timing gaps, weak weeks, uneven collections, or preventable swings.

02

Revenue is up, but margin does not feel stronger.

Customer mix, product mix, pricing lag, freight, and supplier cost changes can quietly compress profit.

03

Costs creep without one obvious cause.

Payroll, overhead, freight, purchasing, and operating habits can each add pressure without one clean culprit.

What the pilot diagnostic looks for

Three areas drive the diagnostic read.

The pilot is not a generic dashboard setup. It is a focused owner-level review of cash control, revenue and profit quality, and lost profit or inflated cost pressure.

Cash Control

Looks at cash movement, timing pressure, cash floor risk, and short-term visibility where the data supports it.

  • Cash movement patterns
  • Timing pressure
  • Cash floor risk

Revenue & Profit

Looks at what is actually driving sales, gross profit, customer concentration, and margin strength.

  • Revenue concentration
  • Customer and product mix
  • Profit driver signals

Lost Profit / Inflated Cost

Looks for quiet pressure from margin leakage, pricing gaps, freight give-back, payroll creep, overhead growth, and controllable cost movement.

  • Margin leakage
  • Freight and pricing pressure
  • Controllable cost movement

Why this is a pilot

A structured exchange, not a general free consulting offer.

Margin Mill is currently running a limited pilot phase to test and refine the diagnostic process with real industrial distribution businesses.

The pilot is a structured exchange: diagnostic work in return for feedback, process refinement, and possible anonymized proof of useful findings.

This keeps the engagement serious. It is not a casual review, a free spreadsheet cleanup, or an open-ended advisory relationship.

What a useful diagnostic depends on

Perfect data is not required. Usable data is.

Margin Mill does not need perfect systems or perfect data. But the diagnostic does need enough usable information to produce a grounded read.

A useful pilot depends on basic business context, usable exports, and a review conversation to confirm what the numbers appear to show.

  • Basic company context
  • Usable exports from available systems
  • A short fit conversation before analysis begins
  • A review conversation after the diagnostic is complete
  • Honest feedback on whether the findings were useful
  • Permission to use anonymized patterns or results only where appropriate
Data use & confidentiality

Company-identifying details stay protected unless permission is given.

Data is used for the diagnostic. Findings are based on client-provided information, and data quality limits will be clearly stated.

Internal use

Client data is used to produce the diagnostic and related review. The analysis is limited by the completeness and accuracy of the exports provided.

Public proof

Public examples are anonymized unless explicit written permission is given. Margin Mill may reference business patterns, not company-identifying details, without permission.
